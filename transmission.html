TRANSMISSION

  • Through a mosquito bite.
    Transmission happens through the bite of an infected mosquito of the Aedes species. These mosquitos live indoors and outdoors and prefer to live near people. They bite mostly during the day, but can also be active at night.
  • From mother to child.
    Infected mothers can transmit the virus to the child during birth. The disease can also be passed to the fetus during pregnancy.
  • Through sexual contact.
    Men can spread the disease through sexual contact. The virus stays longer in semen than in blood.
  • Through blood transfusion.
    No cases of transmission through blood transfusion have been identified in the United States. But cases have been reported in Brazil and French Polynesian.

SYMPTOMS

Fever
Rash
Joint Pain
Red Eyes
